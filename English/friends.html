Friends: For Alpha's and Beta's

School is way easier when you’ve got good friends by your side. But making friends, keeping them, and figuring out who’s actually there for you? That’s the tricky part. Whether you’re the social butterfly or the quiet observer, this guide will help you navigate the world of friendship's.

How to Make Friends

Making friends isn’t about being the most popular person in school—it’s about finding people you actually connect with.

Here’s how:

🗣 1. Talk to People (Even If It’s Awkward at First)

🎭 2. Be Yourself (Seriously)

🎮 3. Join a Club, Sport, or Activity

🤝 4. Be a Good Listener

😆 5. Don’t Force It

How Many Friends Do You Need?

There’s no magic number. Some people thrive with a huge friend group, others are happier with just one or two close friends. Here’s the breakdown:

  • Big Friend Groups – More social events, more variety, but sometimes more drama.
  • Small Circles – Deeper connections, more trust, but fewer people to rely on.
  • A Mix of Both – Some casual friends, a few close ones—best of both worlds.

At the end of the day, quality > quantity. A few real friends are better than a hundred fake ones.

✅ True Friends:

✔ Support you even when things aren’t going great.

✔ Keep your secrets and don’t talk behind your back.

✔ Respect your boundaries and don’t pressure you into things.

✔ Are happy for your success, not jealous.

❌ Fake Friends:

✘ Only talk to you when they need something.

✘ Spread rumors or gossip about you.

✘ Make you feel bad, embarrassed, or left out.

✘ Disappear when you need them most.

If someone constantly brings negativity into your life, they’re not a real friend. It’s okay to let go of toxic friendships.

Final Thought: Friends Make School Better

Good friends make school bearable—even fun. There for the inside jokes, the tough days, and everything in between. Choose them wisely, be a good friend yourself, and remember: it’s about who you have, not how many.
